## Design Context

### Users
Dispatchers and managers using the app on mobile in operational contexts — assigning volunteers, checking duty vehicles, managing shifts. Secondary users are volunteers checking their own schedules. Fast, task-focused sessions with little patience for friction.

### Brand Personality
Official · Young · Cool — the energy of a youth emergency service: authoritative enough to be trusted, dynamic enough to attract young volunteers. Not bureaucratic. Not playful. Somewhere between a modern government app and a consumer sports/activity app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode only. Brand red `#E30613` (MDA official red) as the primary accent. Rubik font — good RTL Hebrew support, modern, slightly rounded. Clean and dense enough for operational data. No dark mode. No glassmorphism overload. Should feel like a well-crafted Israeli government mobile app crossed with a modern scheduling tool.

Anti-references: avoid corporate gray SaaS, avoid generic "startup" purple/blue palettes, avoid playful/childish aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. **Operational first** — every screen should enable fast task completion. No decorative friction.
2. **RTL native** — Hebrew text, right-to-left layout, Israeli date/time conventions throughout.
3. **Mobile dispatcher** — primary viewport is a phone screen held by someone making quick decisions.
4. **Authority + energy** — the red signals urgency and MDA identity; everything else supports it cleanly.
5. **Data density over decoration** — show the information, skip the chrome.
